# Finding Your Services in Railway

## üéØ You're Currently On: Workspace Settings

You're on the **"General Settings"** page (workspace level). You need to go to your **Project** to see your services.

## ‚úÖ Steps to Find Your Services

### Step 1: Go to Your Project

1. Look at the **top left** of the Railway page
2. You should see **"tredoux555's Projects"** or a project name
3. Click on it, or look for a **"Projects"** link in the sidebar
4. You should see a list of projects
5. Click on **"guardian-connect"** (or your project name)

### Step 2: View Your Services

Once in your project, you'll see:
- **Backend service** (if deployed)
- **Frontend service** (if deployed)
- **PostgreSQL database** (if added)

### Step 3: Get Your URLs

**Option A: From Service Card**
1. Click on a service (e.g., "Backend" or "Frontend")
2. The service card shows the URL at the top
3. Click the URL to open it

**Option B: From Settings**
1. Click on a service
2. Go to **"Settings"** tab
3. Scroll to **"Domains"** section
4. Copy the URL shown

## üîç Quick Navigation

**From where you are now:**
1. Click **"Projects"** in the left sidebar (or top navigation)
2. Click **"guardian-connect"** project
3. You'll see all your services

**Or:**
1. Look for a **back arrow** or **breadcrumb** at the top
2. Click to go back to your project
3. Then click on a service

## üìç What You Should See

In your project view, you'll see cards for:
- **Backend** service (with status, URL, etc.)
- **Frontend** service (if deployed)
- **PostgreSQL** database (if added)

Each service card shows:
- Service name
- Status (Running, Building, etc.)
- URL (clickable)
- Resource usage

## üöÄ If You Don't See Services

If you don't see any services:
1. You might need to create them first
2. Go back to project overview
3. Click **"+ New"** button
4. Select **"GitHub Repo"**
5. Choose your repository
6. Set Root Directory (important!)
